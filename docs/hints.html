You must guess valid five-letter English words, and the goal is to determine the secret word that is part of the answer phrase. When you make a guess, one of four things will appear on the tree:

  • If you guess a word that isn't in the dictionary, the tree will be all red.
  • If you guess a word that is valid, but shares no letters with the answer word, the tree will be fully dark.
  • For each letter that is in the answer word but in the wrong position, a blue ornament will appear.
  • For each letter that is in the answer word and in the correct position, a green ornament will appear.
Order of ornaments on the tree does not matter.

There's a guide in the bottom right of the screen that is intended to give you some helpful clues.

This diagram is trying to tell you to read the edges of the grid, starting on the top left and going clockwise around the entire grid. Read the data in pairs of two. There's also a unique symbol that is a strong clue to how to interpret the data.

There are two steps. You need to turn the colors into numbers (Step 1), and you need to turn the numbers into letters (Step 2).

Recognize that zig-zag symbol in the guide, maybe from physics class?

The zig-zag symbol is a resistor. Resistors have color bands, and those color bands indicate a numeric digit. You can use this scheme to translate the colors to digits.

Interpret the paired digits as letters, with 01 being A and 26 being Z. Make sure to interpret each digit as if you are reading it as you travel around the grid (e.g. digits are read from right to left on the bottom edge.)
